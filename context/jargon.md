# Project Jargon & Terminology

A comprehensive glossary of terms, phrases, and acronyms used in the Mahjong app project.

---

## Mahjong Game Terms

### American Mahjong
The most complicated version of Mahjong, created by Jewish ladies who changed the game from traditional Asian versions. Includes the Charleston and uses the National Mahjong League card system.

### Card / NML Card
The physical card released annually by the National Mahjong League showing all valid "hands" (winning combinations) for that year. Released each April with new patterns. All players must use the same card for fair play.

### Charleston
A series of tile passes that occurs before the game begins. Includes passing tiles right, across, left, and an optional courtesy pass. Takes ~30 minutes to teach in beginner classes. **Major pain point for beginners.**

### Chinese Mahjong
A version of Mahjong played with different rules than American Mahjong. Considered for post-MVP features but not part of initial scope.

### Concealed Hand
A winning hand where tiles are not revealed/exposed to other players until winning. Typically worth more points than exposed hands.

### Consecutive Run
A sequence of tiles in numerical order (e.g., 1-2-3 or 4-5-6). One type of pattern that can appear in valid hands on the NML card.

### Courtesy Pass
The optional final pass in the Charleston sequence, allowing players to exchange tiles with the person opposite them.

### Dealing
The process of distributing tiles to players at the start of a game. One of the core tutorial topics.

### Exposed Hand
A winning hand where tiles are revealed to other players during gameplay (through Pungs, Kongs, etc.). Typically worth fewer points than concealed hands.

### Hand
A valid winning combination of tiles as shown on the National Mahjong League card. Players try to match their tiles to one of the patterns on the card.

### Heavenly Hands
A challenge mode featured on I Love Mahj where players are given specific tiles and must identify which hand/line to play. Helps strengthen "Mahjong eye."

### Kong
A set of four identical tiles. A special move in Mahjong that can be declared during gameplay.

### Line
A specific pattern/combination on the NML card. Each card section contains multiple lines representing different valid hands.

### Mahjong Eye
The ability to quickly recognize patterns and identify which hands are possible given a set of tiles. Developed through practice and experience.

### Messy Mahjong
House rules version of American Mahjong played without the Charleston. Simpler variation sometimes preferred for casual play.

### Pung
A set of three identical tiles. Can be concealed or exposed depending on how it's obtained.

### Quints
Five identical tiles. One type of pattern that can appear in valid hands on the NML card.

### Section
A category of hands on the NML card (e.g., consecutive runs, quints, specific patterns). The card is organized into multiple sections.

### Taiwanese Mahjong
Another version of Mahjong with its own rule set. Considered for post-MVP features but not part of initial scope.

### Tiles
The 152 pieces used in American Mahjong, including bamboos, characters, dots, winds, dragons, flowers, and jokers.

### Walls
The arrangement of tiles at the start of the game. Players build "walls" of tiles in front of them that form a square. One of the core tutorial topics.

## User Personas & Demographics

### Candy Crush Mom
Alternative name for the primary user persona. A woman who spends significant time on mobile games like Candy Crush, willing to invest both time and money in engaging apps. Overlaps with "Pickleball Mom."

### Pickleball Mom

### Traditional Mahjong Player
Secondary persona. Women 60+ who have been playing for 30+ years, very particular about rules, play "by the book." Need very simple, user-friendly interfaces.

### Trendy Young Crowd
Secondary persona. College age to young moms (20s-30s) who love the aesthetic and style aspect of Mahjong. More tech-savvy than primary persona.

## Competitor & Market Terms

### I Love Mahj (ilovemaj.com)
Current market leader for online American Mahjong. Website (not mobile app) charging $6/month subscription. Features tournaments, leaderboards, bot play, and "Heavenly Hands" challenges. Currently recommended by Genny & Rebekah to students.

### Mahj with Friends / Mahjong 4 Friends
Most talked about competitor app in Mahjong groups. Has poor UI and is not user-friendly for beginners despite being popular.

### Mahjongo
Competitor app with the best graphics among competitors, resembling traditional/automatic Mahjong tables. Still has UI issues but less cluttered than others.

### Real Mahjong / Real Mah Jongg
Competitor app. Uses "Mah Jongg" spelling related to NML copyright. Has poor UI.

## Project-Specific Terms

### Bot Opponents
Computer-controlled players that users can practice against. Essential for practice before the app has critical mass of real users. Complexity needs to be assessed vs. online multiplayer.

### Discovery Phase
Initial project phase focused on understanding the game, market, and users. Includes creating context documents, entity relationship blueprints, information architecture, and proof-of-concept prototypes.

### Entity Relationship Blueprint
High-level database model showing how different data entities (users, lessons, games, etc.) relate to each other. Part of the discovery phase deliverables.

### Information Architecture
Document mapping user flows and screen sequences throughout the app. Shows how users navigate from one feature to another.

### MVP (Minimal Viable Product)
The least amount of features needed to launch and generate revenue. Focus on tutorials, Charleston practice, and potentially bot play. Excludes advanced features like tournaments, brand partnerships, and community features.

### V1.5 / V2
Post-MVP versions of the app that would include additional features like brand partnerships, multiple game modes, community features, etc.

## Monetization Terms

### Affiliate Revenue
Commission earned from referring users to purchase physical Mahjong products. Partners could include Oh My Mahjong, smaller Mahjong brands, and Amazon. Instructors could also become affiliates with unique referral codes.

### Annual Card Breakdown
Potential paid content ($9.99) released each April when the new NML card comes out, explaining the new hands and patterns.

### Freemium Model
Business model where the app is free to download with basic features, but users must pay to unlock additional content (lessons, challenges, customization options).

### In-App Purchases / Microtransactions
Optional purchases within the app, such as tile designs, backgrounds, mats, themes, and cosmetic customizations.

### Instructor Referral Program
System where Mahjong instructors receive unique codes/links to share with students. Instructors earn commission when students subscribe using their code. Tracked via software like Tapfiliate.

### Subscription Model
Business model where users pay recurring fees (monthly or annual) for access to the app. Example pricing: $4-6/month or $60-100/year.

### Tapfiliate
Third-party software that tracks affiliate referrals and earnings. Provides dashboard for affiliates to see their commissions and handles automated payouts.

## Gamification Terms

### Achievement System
Trophies, badges, and rewards given to users for completing milestones, lessons, or challenges. Creates "dopamine hits" to drive engagement.

### Confetti / Celebration Animations
Visual rewards displayed when users complete tasks or reach milestones. Used to create positive reinforcement.

### Login Streaks
Snapchat-style system that tracks consecutive days of app usage. Encourages daily engagement through fear of losing the streak.

### Profile Completion Rewards
Points or bonuses given to users for setting up their account and filling out profile information.

### Timed Challenges
Exercises with time limits to help users improve speed. Beginners typically take 45-60 minutes per game; advanced players complete games in 15 minutes.

### Unlock Mechanics
System where users must complete certain actions or reach levels to gain access to new features, customization options, or challenges.

## Technical & Legal Terms

### NML (National Mahjong League)
Organization that standardizes American Mahjong rules and releases the annual card. Copyrighted the spelling "Mahjongg" (with double G). Their card is copyrighted intellectual property.

### NML Copyright Issue
Legal concern about using the official National Mahjong League card in the app. May require permission/licensing or the creation of custom sequences/hands that all players use.

### Custom Card / Custom Sequences
Alternative to using the NML card. Creating original hand patterns that would be used exclusively in the app. Benefit: one-time development with no annual maintenance costs. Risk: Players may prefer official NML card.

## Key Phrases & Concepts

### "Mahjong Instructor in an App"
Core product positioning. The app should function as a personal instructor that users can access anytime to learn and practice.

### "Strengthen Your Mahjong Eye"
Marketing phrase for challenge modes that help users develop pattern recognition skills.

### "Next Bunko" / "Next Pickleball"
Description of Mahjong as a trending activity among the target demographic, suggesting strong market opportunity.

### "By the Book"
Phrase used to describe traditional players who are very particular about following official NML rules exactly.

### "What Hand Am I Playing?"
Core challenge for beginners - identifying which pattern on the NML card to pursue given their tiles.

### "Play Dirty"
Slang for playing strategically/aggressively, such as holding onto tiles other players need or deliberately discarding tiles to mislead opponents.

## Development Terms

### 3D Elements
Potential visual representation of tiles and table in three dimensions. May complicate AI prototype development.

### AI Proof-of-Concept
Attempt to use AI tools to quickly create a clickable prototype. May fall back to traditional flowchart approach if game/3D elements are too complex.

### Online Multiplayer
Feature allowing users to play against other real people over the internet. Complexity needs to be assessed vs. bot opponents.

### Asynchronous Play
Words with Friends-style gameplay where players take turns when convenient rather than playing in real-time.
