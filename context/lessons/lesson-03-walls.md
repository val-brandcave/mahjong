1. Understand what "walls" are and their purpose in Mahjong
2. Know how to build a wall (stacking tiles)

## Lesson Overview

This lesson teaches users how to build the physical walls that form the tile structure before dealing in Mahjong. It's a hands-on, practical lesson focused on the mechanical setup of the game. Users will learn through visual demonstrations, step-by-step instructions, and interactive practice.

**Tone:** Instructional but encouraging, like teaching a physical skill
**Approach:** Visual demonstration, step-by-step guidance, interactive practice
**Key Message:** "Building walls looks complicated, but it's just stacking tiles in a pattern. You've got this!"

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. What Are Walls?
- Walls are tiles stacked face-down at the start of the game
- Form a square in the center of the table
- Each player builds their own wall in front of them
- Walls are pushed together to create one large square

### 2. Wall Dimensions
- **Length:** 19 tiles long
- **Height:** 2 tiles high (stacked)
- **Total per wall:** 38 tiles (19 √ó 2)
- **All four walls:** 152 tiles total (38 √ó 4)

### 3. How to Stack Tiles
- Tiles placed face-down
- Two tiles stacked on top of each other
- Create pairs of stacked tiles
- 19 pairs arranged in a line

### 4. Directional Positions
- **East:** Starting position (dealer)
- **South:** To East's right
- **West:** Across from East
- **North:** To East's left
- Counter-clockwise seating arrangement

### 5. Pushing Walls Together
- Each player builds their wall in front of them
- Walls are pushed toward center
- Form a square "cage" of tiles
- Small gap between corners

### 6. Breaking the Wall
- After building, one wall will be "broken" for dealing
- East rolls dice to determine where to break
- Tiles are dealt from the break point
- (Detailed in next lesson - just introduce concept here)
