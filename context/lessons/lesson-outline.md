# Mahjong App - Lesson Outline

A comprehensive outline of all tutorial lessons in the app, organized by learning progression and priority.

---

## Lesson Structure Overview

**Total Lessons:** 10-12 lessons (comprehensive coverage)
**MVP Lessons:** 4-5 core lessons
**Lesson Duration:** 3-10 minutes each
**Format:** Interactive practice with brief text/video introductions
**Progression:** Sequential unlocking (beginner path) with review access

---

## Learning Path Architecture

### Phase 1: Foundation (MVP Core)
Getting started with Mahjong basics - what the game is, what you need, and how it works.

### Phase 2: Setup & Structure (MVP Core)
Understanding the physical setup of the game before playing.

### Phase 3: The Charleston (MVP Core - HIGHEST PRIORITY)
Mastering the most intimidating and confusing part for beginners.

### Phase 4: Reading the Card (MVP Core)
Learning to understand and use the National Mahjong League card.

### Phase 5: Basic Gameplay (MVP Optional / V2)
Actually playing the game - turns, discards, and winning.

### Phase 6: Strategy & Advanced (V2)
Moving beyond basics to strategy and competitive play.

### **LESSON 01: Welcome to Mahjong**
**Phase:** Foundation
**Priority:** MVP Core
**Duration:** 3-5 minutes
**Difficulty:** Beginner

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand what Mahjong is and why people play it
- Learn the basic goal of the game
- Get familiar with American Mahjong vs. other versions
- Build excitement and confidence

#### Topics Covered
- What is American Mahjong?
- Brief history (Jewish ladies created this version)
- The goal: Match your tiles to a pattern on the card
- Why it's fun and social
- What makes it different from Asian versions

#### Teaching Approach
- Welcome video/animation (30-60 seconds)
- Simple infographic showing the goal
- Friendly, encouraging tone
- No complex terminology yet
- End with motivation: "You can learn this!"

#### Interactive Elements
- Simple quiz: "What is the goal of Mahjong?" (multiple choice)
- Visual: Show example of winning hand vs. tiles
- Tap to explore: Different versions of Mahjong (locked for now)

#### Prerequisites
- None (first lesson)

#### Unlocks

### **LESSON 02: Know Your Tiles**
**Phase:** Foundation
**Priority:** MVP Core
**Duration:** 5-8 minutes
**Difficulty:** Beginner

#### Learning Objectives
- Identify all tile types (Bamboo, Character, Dot, Winds, Dragons, Flowers, Jokers)
- Understand tile quantities (how many of each)
- Recognize tile symbols and numbers
- Learn basic tile terminology

#### Topics Covered
- The 152 tiles in American Mahjong
- Suits: Bamboo (Bams), Characters (Craks), Dots
- Honor tiles: Winds (East, South, West, North)
- Dragons: Red, Green, White/Soap
- Flowers and Jokers (wild cards)
- Tile quantities and distribution

#### Teaching Approach
- Visual tile gallery with clear labels
- Interactive tile matching exercises
- Memory game elements (flip and match)
- Simple, clear categories
- Focus on recognition, not memorization

#### Interactive Elements
- **Exercise 1:** Tap tiles to hear their names and see categories
- **Exercise 2:** "Which of these is a Bamboo?" (drag & drop sorting)
- **Exercise 3:** "How many Red Dragons are there?" (multiple choice)
- **Exercise 4:** Match the tile to its name (5 tiles)
- **Challenge:** Find all the Jokers (tap to identify)

### **LESSON 04: The Deal**
**Phase:** Setup & Structure
**Priority:** MVP Core
**Duration:** 4-6 minutes
**Difficulty:** Beginner

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand how tiles are dealt to players
- Learn the dealing sequence and pattern
- Know how many tiles each player receives
- Understand who deals and who goes first (East wind)

#### Topics Covered
- East player rolls dice to determine where to break the wall
- Dealing sequence: East deals first
- Each player gets 13 tiles (East gets 14)
- Dealing pattern: 4 tiles at a time, then final tiles
- Organizing your rack
- Face-down dealing (tiles hidden from other players)

#### Teaching Approach
- Animated dealing demonstration
- Follow-along dealing practice
- Number highlighting (counting tiles)
- Clear sequence steps (1, 2, 3...)

#### Interactive Elements
- **Exercise 1:** Watch dealing animation
- **Exercise 2:** "How many tiles does East start with?" (multiple choice)
- **Exercise 3:** Practice dealing sequence (tap in correct order)

### **LESSON 05: Charleston Part 1 - The Right Pass**
**Phase:** The Charleston (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
**Priority:** MVP Core - CRITICAL
**Duration:** 6-8 minutes
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand what the Charleston is and why it exists
- Learn the first pass: PASS RIGHT
- Practice selecting tiles to pass
- Build confidence with the most intimidating part of the game

#### Topics Covered
- What is the Charleston? (tile-passing before play)
- Why do we do it? (organize tiles, build hands)
- First Charleston: 3 passes required
- Pass Right: Select 3 tiles to pass to player on your right
- Strategy basics: What to pass (tiles you don't want)
- All players pass simultaneously

#### Teaching Approach
- Break Charleston into digestible chunks (Part 1 of 3)
- Reassuring tone: "This seems scary but it's just passing tiles!"
- Clear directional indicators (arrows pointing RIGHT)
- Step-by-step guided practice
- Positive reinforcement after each successful pass

#### Interactive Elements
- **Exercise 1:** Identify the player on your right (tap player)
- **Exercise 2:** Select 3 tiles from your hand (tap to select)
- **Exercise 3:** Pass tiles right (drag tiles to player)
- **Exercise 4:** Receive tiles from your left (automatic)
- **Exercise 5:** Practice complete right pass (3 rounds)
- **Challenge:** Complete right pass in under 30 seconds

#### Prerequisites
- Lesson 04: The Deal

#### Unlocks
- Lesson 06: Charleston Part 2

### **LESSON 06: Charleston Part 2 - Across and Left**
**Phase:** The Charleston (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
**Priority:** MVP Core - CRITICAL
**Duration:** 7-10 minutes
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

#### Learning Objectives
- Learn the second pass: PASS ACROSS
- Learn the third pass: PASS LEFT
- Complete the First Charleston sequence
- Understand the full pass pattern (Right ‚Üí Across ‚Üí Left)

#### Topics Covered
- Pass Across: Select 3 tiles to pass to player opposite you
- Pass Left: Select 3 tiles to pass to player on your left
- Full sequence: Right ‚Üí Across ‚Üí Left
- This is called the "First Charleston"
- Reviewing tiles after each pass
- All passes are mandatory (must pass 3 tiles)

#### Teaching Approach
- Build on Part 1 knowledge
- Clear directional indicators (arrows pointing ACROSS, then LEFT)
- Repeat the pattern multiple times
- Memory aid: "Right, Across, Left" (RAL)
- Celebrate completion of First Charleston

#### Interactive Elements
- **Exercise 1:** Review Right pass (quick practice)
- **Exercise 2:** Identify player across from you (tap player)
- **Exercise 3:** Pass across (select and drag 3 tiles)
- **Exercise 4:** Identify player on your left (tap player)
- **Exercise 5:** Pass left (select and drag 3 tiles)
- **Exercise 6:** Complete full First Charleston (Right ‚Üí Across ‚Üí Left)
- **Challenge:** Complete First Charleston in under 60 seconds

### **LESSON 08: Understanding the Card**
**Phase:** Reading the Card
**Priority:** MVP Core
**Duration:** 8-10 minutes
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand what the National Mahjong League card is
- Learn how to read the card sections
- Identify different hand patterns
- Choose a hand to pursue
- Understand concealed vs. exposed indicators

#### Topics Covered
- What is the NML card? (All valid winning hands)
- Card changes annually (released each April)
- Card sections: Consecutive Runs, Quints, Singles & Pairs, etc.
- Reading a line: Understanding symbols and patterns
- C = Concealed, X = Exposed
- Numbers and suits (specific tiles needed)
- Point values (harder hands = more points)
- Choosing a realistic hand for your tiles

#### Teaching Approach
- Visual card with zoom capability
- Color coding for different sections
- Symbol legend (always visible)
- Example hands broken down step-by-step
- Focus on 2-3 simple hands first
- Progressive complexity

#### Interactive Elements
- **Exercise 1:** Explore the card sections (tap to expand)
- **Exercise 2:** "What does this symbol mean?" (multiple choice)
- **Exercise 3:** Match tiles to a simple hand (drag & drop)
- **Exercise 4:** Identify concealed vs. exposed indicators
- **Exercise 5:** "Which hand can you make?" (given tiles, choose hand)
- **Exercise 6:** Practice reading 3 different hand patterns
- **Challenge:** Find the highest-point hand you can make

### **LESSON 09: Gameplay Basics**
**Phase:** Basic Gameplay
**Priority:** MVP Optional / V2
**Duration:** 10-12 minutes
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand turn sequence (draw and discard)
- Learn how to call tiles (for exposing hands)
- Know when you can and cannot call
- Recognize dead hands and invalid moves
- Understand turn rotation

#### Topics Covered
- East starts the game
- Turn sequence: Draw a tile ‚Üí Discard a tile
- Calling tiles: "Mahjong!", "Call!", etc.
- When can you call? (Someone discards tile you need)
- Exposing tiles when you call
- You cannot call for concealed hands
- Dead hand: Mistakes that disqualify you
- Turn rotation: Right to left (counterclockwise)
- Game continues until someone wins or wall is depleted

#### Teaching Approach
- Step-by-step gameplay walkthrough
- Visual indicators for active player
- Highlighting available actions
- Practice scenarios with decision points
- Error prevention (system prevents illegal moves)

#### Interactive Elements
- **Exercise 1:** Practice drawing and discarding (5 turns)
- **Exercise 2:** "Can you call this tile?" (yes/no scenarios)
- **Exercise 3:** Expose tiles after calling (drag to reveal)
- **Exercise 4:** Identify whose turn it is (tap player)
- **Exercise 5:** Play through 10 turns successfully
- **Challenge:** Complete a full simulated game

#### Prerequisites
- Lesson 08: Understanding the Card

#### Unlocks
- Lesson 10: Winning & Scoring

- Lessons unlock sequentially (can't skip ahead)
