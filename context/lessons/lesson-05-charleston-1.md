## Lesson Overview

**THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT LESSON IN THE APP.** The Charleston is the #1 pain point for beginners - it's intimidating, confusing, and takes 30+ minutes to teach in person. This lesson breaks it down into manageable pieces, starting with just the RIGHT PASS. The tone must be reassuring and encouraging, breaking down fears and building confidence step-by-step.

**Tone:** Reassuring, encouraging, patient, breaking down complexity
**Approach:** Small steps, lots of practice, positive reinforcement, address fears directly
**Key Message:** "The Charleston seems scary, but it's just passing tiles. We'll take it slow, one pass at a time. You've got this!"

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. What is the Charleston?
- Tile-passing phase BEFORE the game begins
- Allows players to organize and improve their tiles
- Everyone participates simultaneously
- It has specific rules and patterns

### 2. Why Does It Exist?
- Get rid of tiles you don't want
- Get tiles you do want (from others)
- Start looking for patterns on the card
- Give everyone a fair chance to improve their hand

### 3. The First Pass: PASS RIGHT
- Select 3 tiles you don't want
- Pass them to the player on your RIGHT
- Simultaneously receive 3 tiles from your LEFT
- All players pass at the same time

### 4. What Tiles to Pass
- Tiles that don't match patterns on your card
- Single tiles with no pairs
- Tiles you have too many of (unless building a specific hand)
- Generally: tiles you can't use

### 5. Charleston Etiquette
- All players pass simultaneously
- Face-down passing (hidden from others)
- No talking about what you're passing
- Wait for everyone to be ready

## Special Notes for Developers & Designers

**THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT LESSON:**

The Charleston is why users will download the app. If we don't nail this lesson, the entire app fails its primary purpose.

**Design Priorities:**
1. **Reduce intimidation at all costs**
2. **Break down into smallest possible pieces**
3. **Provide excessive amounts of practice**
4. **Constant encouragement and reassurance**
5. **Make direction crystal clear (right vs. left)**
6. **Celebrate every small win**

**User Psychology:**
- Users arrive at this lesson anxious and intimidated
- Many have heard Charleston is "the hard part"
- They expect to be confused
- Our job: Prove them wrong, build confidence, make it simple

**Success Metrics:**
- Completion rate above 85%
- Confidence rating increase of +2 points minimum
- User feedback: "That wasn't as bad as I thought!"
