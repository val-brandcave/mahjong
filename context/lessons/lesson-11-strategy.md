## Learning Objectives

1. Learn how to evaluate tiles after Charleston
2. Choose realistic hands based on tiles you have
3. Understand flexibility vs. commitment
4. Recognize when to pivot to a different hand
5. Develop "Mahjong eye" for pattern recognition
6. Make strategic decisions about which hand to pursue

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. Evaluating Your Tiles
- Count what you have (pairs, sets of 3-4, singles)
- Look for patterns (consecutive numbers, matching suits)
- Identify Jokers and how many you have
- Note Dragons and Winds
- Consider Flowers

### 2. Finding Possible Hands
- Scan card sections that match your tiles
- Identify 2-3 possible hands initially
- Compare difficulty (how many tiles do you need?)
- Consider point values (risk vs. reward)

### 3. Staying Flexible
- Don't commit to one hand immediately
- Keep options open early in the game
- Draw and discard to improve options
- Watch what tiles are being discarded

### 4. When to Commit
- Halfway through game, narrow to 1-2 hands
- Commit when you're 3-4 tiles away
- Once committed, discard tiles for other hands
- Stay focused on your chosen pattern

### 5. When to Pivot
- Your tiles aren't coming (tiles you need are dead/discarded)
- You're not getting closer
- Another hand becomes more realistic
- Better to pivot early than late

### 6. Reading Opponents' Discards
- What tiles are being thrown?
- What patterns might opponents be playing?
- Are your needed tiles still available?
- Adjust strategy based on discards

### Screen 4: Comparing Options
**Text:**
```
Which Hand Should You Choose?

Consider these factors:

1. **How close are you?**
   - Need 2 tiles? Very doable!
   - Need 6+ tiles? Might be too hard

2. **Are tiles available?**
   - Look at discards - are your needed tiles gone?
   - If yes, choose different hand

3. **Point value**
   - Higher points = harder but more reward
   - Lower points = easier but less reward
   - Beginners: Choose easier hands (25-40 points)

4. **Jokers**
   - Jokers can substitute for most tiles
   - More Jokers = more flexibility
```
