## Topics Covered

### 1. What is the NML Card?
- Shows ALL valid winning hand patterns
- Released annually by National Mahjong League (changes every April)
- All players must use the same year's card
- Physical card or digital version
- Essential for playing - you cannot win without matching a card pattern

### 2. Card Sections
- **2024:** (Year displayed at top)
- **Singles & Pairs**
- **Consecutive Runs**
- **Quints** (5 of a kind)
- **3-369** (specific number patterns)
- **Winds & Dragons**
- **Year Hands** (special hands for the year)
- Each section has multiple lines/patterns

### 3. Reading a Line
- Each line = one valid winning hand
- Symbols represent tiles:
  - **FF** = Flowers (any 2)
  - **1111** = Four 1s (any suit)
  - **DDD** = Three Dragons (any combination)
  - **DDDD** = Four Dragons (any combination)
  - **NNNN** = Four matching numbers
  - **NNN** = Three matching numbers
- Numbers can be any suit unless specified

### 4. Concealed vs. Exposed
- **C** = Concealed (tiles stay hidden in your rack)
- **X** = Exposed (tiles revealed to other players)
- **No letter** = Can be either concealed or exposed
- Concealed hands usually worth more points

### 5. Point Values
- Numbers on left of each line show points
- Higher points = harder hands
- Beginners should aim for lower-point hands (easier)
- Points determine who pays winner and how much

### 6. Choosing a Hand
- After Charleston, look at your tiles
- Find patterns on card that match tiles you have
- Choose 1-2 possible hands to pursue
- Stay flexible early - commit to one hand as you get closer
