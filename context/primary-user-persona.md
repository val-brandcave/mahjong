## Quick Summary

Linda is a trend-driven woman in her 50s who recently discovered Mahjong after years of playing Bunko. She's willing to spend both time and money on hobbies she enjoys, much like she does with mobile games like Candy Crush. While not particularly tech-savvy, she's comfortable with her smartphone and downloads apps her friends recommend. She takes beginner Mahjong classes but struggles to retain the complicated rules and needs additional practice between lessons to build confidence.

## Design Implications

### Visual Design
- **Color Palette:** Bright, cheerful colors (reference: Oh My Mahjong)
- **Typography:** Large, legible fonts (minimum 16pt for body text)
- **Buttons:** Extra-large touch targets (minimum 44x44pt)
- **Spacing:** Generous whitespace, not crowded
- **Images:** High-quality, beautiful tile and table visuals

### UX Design
- **Navigation:** Maximum 5 main options on home screen
- **Onboarding:** Guided tour with clear next steps
- **Tutorials:** Interactive practice, not just videos or text
- **Feedback:** Immediate visual confirmation of correct actions
- **Error Handling:** Prevent mistakes rather than correcting them

### Content Strategy
- **Text:** Minimal, concise, friendly tone
- **Instructions:** Short sentences, bullet points
- **Videos:** 30-60 seconds maximum, not long lectures
- **Help:** Contextual tips, not separate help section
- **Terminology:** Avoid jargon, explain terms when used

### Engagement Strategy
- **Progress Visible:** Clear indication of advancement
- **Rewards:** Frequent positive reinforcement
- **Streaks:** Daily login incentives
- **Achievements:** Celebrate small wins with confetti
- **Customization:** Unlock pretty designs as rewards

## Marketing Insights

### How to Reach Her
- **Instructor Recommendations:** Most trusted source
- **Friend Referrals:** Strong word-of-mouth influence
- **Facebook Groups:** Active in Mahjong communities (20,000+ members)
- **Instagram:** Follows Mahjong influencers and brands
- **Class Flyers:** At venues where classes are held

### Messaging That Resonates
- "Practice Mahjong between lessons"
- "Build confidence with step-by-step tutorials"
- "Master the Charleston with guided practice"
- "Learn at your own pace"
- "Your personal Mahjong instructor"
- "Beautiful, simple, easy to use"

### Messaging to Avoid
- Technical jargon or complex terminology
- "Advanced features" or "competitive play" (intimidating)
- References to other complicated games
- Assumptions about tech skills
- Emphasis on speed or competition (stress-inducing)
